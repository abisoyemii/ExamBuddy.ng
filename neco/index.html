About NECO

What is NECO?

NECO (National Examinations Council) is a Nigerian government examination body established in 1999. It conducts the Senior Secondary Certificate Examination (SSCE) for students in Nigerian secondary schools.

NECO is conducted entirely within Nigeria, unlike WAEC which is a West African body. Both NECO and WAEC results are accepted for university admissions and employment in Nigeria.

NECO is generally considered slightly less competitive than WAEC in terms of difficulty, though this varies by subject and year. Many students sit both WAEC and NECO to strengthen their O-Level results.

Study Tips

6 Strategies to Pass NECO in 2026

These strategies are tailored specifically for NECO — not just copied from WAEC advice.

01

Use NECO-Specific Past Questions

Don't rely only on WAEC past questions. NECO has its own style and question patterns. Practise NECO past questions to understand the format and typical question phrasing.

02

Focus on Theory Questions

NECO's essay/theory papers carry significant marks. Write clearly, structure your answers, and cover all required points. Practise writing under timed conditions.

03

Pay Attention to Practical Work

Science practicals are assessed seriously in NECO. Know your experiment procedures, how to present results in tables and graphs, and how to write conclusions correctly.

04

Don't Neglect Continuous Assessment

NECO often includes continuous assessment marks. Consistent performance throughout your school year contributes to your final grade, so don't ignore tests and assignments.

05

Download the Official Syllabus

Get the official NECO syllabus for each subject. Study only what's within it. Download it from the official NECO website or our Resources page.

06

Prepare for Agricultural Science

Agricultural Science is a popular NECO subject. Focus on crop production, soil science, farm management, and animal husbandry — these topics carry the most marks.
