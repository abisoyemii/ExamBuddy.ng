What is IELTS?

IELTS Overview

The International English Language Testing System (IELTS) is the world's most popular English language proficiency test, accepted by over 11,000 organisations in 140+ countries — including universities in the UK, Canada, Australia, USA, and Ireland.

There are two versions: IELTS Academic (for university admission and professional registration) and IELTS General Training (for work visas, migration, and secondary education). Most Nigerian students applying to foreign universities need the Academic version.

IELTS is jointly administered in Nigeria by the British Council and IDP Education. Register only through their official websites.

Registration

How to Register for IELTS in Nigeria

Always register through official channels only — British Council Nigeria or IDP Nigeria.

1

Choose Your Test Type

Decide between IELTS Academic (university admission) or IELTS General Training (work/migration). Also choose between Paper-Based Test (PBT) or Computer-Based Test (CBT). CBT gives faster results and more available dates.

2

Choose Your Test Centre

Nigeria has test centres in Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt, Kano, Enugu, Ibadan and more. Visit britishcouncil.org.ng or idp.com/nigeria to see available dates near you.

3

Create an Account & Register Online

Go to the British Council or IDP website, create an account using your valid international passport details, select your preferred test date and centre, then proceed to registration.

4

Pay the Registration Fee

The fee is approximately ₦130,000–₦150,000 (subject to exchange rate changes). You can pay online via card or bank transfer. Keep your payment receipt.

5

Receive Confirmation & Prepare Your ID

You'll receive a booking confirmation by email. On test day, bring the same valid international passport you used during registration — no other ID is accepted.

6

Take the Test & Receive Results

Paper-based results are released 13 days after the test. Computer-based results arrive within 3–5 days. You can send results directly to institutions from your online account.

⚠️ Avoid third parties claiming to offer discounted IELTS registration. Only register at britishcouncil.org.ng or idp.com/nigeria.
Test Sections

The Four IELTS Sections Explained

Understand what each section tests and how to perform well as a Nigerian student.

🎧

Listening — 30 Minutes

4 recordings (conversations and monologues), 40 questions. The main challenge for Nigerian students is British and Australian accents.

💡 Tip: Practice with BBC World Service podcasts and IELTS Cambridge books daily to train your ear.
📖

Reading — 60 Minutes

3 long academic passages, 40 questions. Tests skimming, scanning, and detail comprehension. Many students run out of time.

💡 Tip: Aim for 20 minutes per passage. Practice reading speed with timed exercises daily.
✍️

Writing — 60 Minutes

Task 1: Describe a graph/chart (150 words, 20 min). Task 2: Essay (250 words, 40 min). Task 2 carries more marks.

💡 Tip: Master academic essay structure — Introduction, 2 body paragraphs, Conclusion. Practice one essay every 2 days.
🗣️

Speaking — 11–14 Minutes

Face-to-face interview with an examiner. 3 parts: Introduction, a 2-minute talk on a topic card, and a broader discussion.

💡 Tip: Don't memorise scripts — examiners can tell. Practice fluent, natural conversation and record yourself to review.

Study Strategy

8-Week IELTS Preparation Plan

A realistic plan for Nigerian students targeting Band 6.5–7.0.

W1–2

Diagnostic & Foundation

Take a full mock test first to know your baseline score. Get the Cambridge IELTS practice books — these are the most authentic materials available. Understand the format of each section thoroughly.

W3–4

Listening & Reading Intensive

Complete one full Listening + Reading practice daily. Listen to BBC World Service, CNN, or TED Talks to train your ear for different accents. Build reading speed with timed exercises.

W5–6

Writing Drills

Practice one Task 1 and one Task 2 essay every 2 days. Study academic vocabulary. Use essay templates as a guide but always write in your own voice — don't copy templates word for word.

W7

Speaking Practice

Practice speaking out loud every day — record yourself on your phone and listen back. Join an IELTS speaking partner group on WhatsApp or Telegram. Focus on fluency, not just correctness.

W8

Full Mock Tests & Review

Take 2–3 full timed mock tests this week. Review every mistake carefully. Rest properly 2 days before the exam — cramming at the last minute hurts Speaking and Writing performance.
