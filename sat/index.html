SAT Overview

What the SAT Is and How It Works

The SAT measures reading, writing, and math skills used in college admission. This guide helps you understand each section and prepare with confidence.

📚

Test Structure

The SAT has two main sections: Evidence-Based Reading & Writing and Math. Each section is scored from 200 to 800, for a total score between 400 and 1600.

2 sections
⏱️

Timing

The standard test lasts 3 hours. You will answer 154 questions with no guessing penalty, so accuracy and pacing are both essential.

3 hours

Why It Matters

SAT scores can strengthen your application for international scholarships, study abroad programs, and private university entry in Nigeria.

Preparation Tips

SAT Study Tips for Every Candidate

Use these preparation strategies to improve your score efficiently and stay calm on the day of the exam.

01

Understand the Concepts

SAT success comes from understanding why answers are correct. Focus on concept mastery rather than memorizing shortcuts.

02

Review Every Mistake

Log your incorrect answers and review them carefully. Learn the mistake pattern and avoid repeating it.

03

Build Timed Endurance

Take full-length tests under timed conditions. Realistic practice helps you manage energy and avoid rushing near the end.

04

Master Grammar Rules

Writing & Language questions reward quick recognition of sentence structure and punctuation. Study common grammar rules daily.

05

Read Actively

Annotate SAT passages and identify main ideas before answering. Active reading helps you answer faster and more accurately.

06

Use Your Calculator Wisely

Know when the calculator helps and when mental math is faster. Practice with the same calculator model you will use on test day.

FAQ

Frequently Asked SAT Questions

Quick answers to the questions most students ask before their SAT exam.

What is the best way to start SAT preparation?

Begin with a diagnostic test to identify your strengths and weaknesses. Then build a study plan around the section where you need the most improvement.

How many practice tests should I take before the exam?

Take at least 3 full-length practice tests under timed conditions. Use them to adjust pacing, refine strategies, and build exam-day endurance.

Should I take the SAT with or without the essay?

Most schools no longer require the SAT essay. Confirm your target programs first, but plan for the standard test unless the school explicitly asks for the essay.

Can I improve my score in one month?

Yes, with focused practice and daily review. Prioritize the sections with the largest score gaps and take at least two timed tests before your exam.

What should I bring to the SAT test centre?

Bring your valid ID, admission ticket, approved calculator, pencils, eraser, and snacks for after the exam. Arrive early and check the test center rules ahead of time.
