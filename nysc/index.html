Overview

The 3 Phases of NYSC Service Year

Understanding the full structure helps you plan and make the most of each phase.

🏕️

Phase 1 — Orientation Camp

3 weeks at a state camp. Activities include drills, lectures, sports, Community Development Service (CDS) registration, and skills acquisition workshops.

3 Weeks
🏢

Phase 2 — PPA (Place of Primary Assignment)

11 months at your assigned workplace. You serve in schools, hospitals, government ministries, or private organisations. Community Development Service continues monthly.

11 Months
🎓

Phase 3 — Passing Out

The final parade ceremony where you receive your NYSC discharge certificate — one of the most important documents for employment in Nigeria.

Discharge Certificate
Getting Started

How to Get Your NYSC Call-Up Letter

Follow these steps carefully — errors in your data are difficult to correct after submission.

1

Senate/School Uploads Your Data

Your institution uploads your data to the NYSC portal. Ensure your name, date of birth, and course are correct on your transcript before they do this.

2

Create Your NYSC Portal Account

Visit portal.nysc.gov.ng and create an account using your matriculation number, email, and personal details. Upload a recent passport photograph.

3

Complete Online Mobilisation

Fill in your personal details, upload required documents (passport, certificate, transcript), and complete the online mobilisation form carefully — errors are difficult to fix.

4

Print Your Call-Up Letter

Once approved, your call-up letter will be available for download. Print multiple copies. This letter specifies your camp state and reporting date.

📋

Documents to Upload

  • ✓Degree/HND certificate or statement of result
  • ✓Academic transcript
  • ✓Birth certificate or age declaration
  • ✓Passport photograph (white background)
  • ✓Local Government Area (LGA) of origin letter
  • ✓Marriage certificate if name changed (women)
⚠️ Ensure all names match across all documents. Discrepancies cause significant delays.

Pro Tips

6 Camp Survival Tips from Corps Members

Advice gathered from hundreds of corps members who have been through camp.

01

Register for the Right CDS Group

Community Development Service groups meet weekly throughout your service year. Choose one aligned with your career goals — education, health, agriculture, tech, or business.

02

Be Early for Everything

Camp activities start at 5am. Get to assembly ground early. Being late leads to punishment. Set an alarm and sleep early — camp is exhausting for the first few days.

03

Protect Your Documents

Keep all documents in a waterproof file or bag. Never leave originals unattended. Losing your call-up letter or certificate at camp creates major complications.

04

Network Seriously

You'll meet future business partners, employers, and lifelong friends at camp. Exchange contacts, join WhatsApp groups for your state, and stay in touch after camp ends.

05

Take Skills Acquisition Seriously

NYSC skills acquisition workshops teach tailoring, bead-making, catering, digital skills, and more. These are genuinely useful and can become side income during your service year.

06

Stay Healthy

Camp conditions can cause malaria and stomach issues. Take prophylactic malaria drugs, use mosquito nets, drink bottled water, and rest when you feel unwell — the medical centre is free.
