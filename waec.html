Exam Format

Understanding the WAEC Paper Structure

✏️

Paper 1 — Objective

Multiple choice questions. Typically 50–60 questions to be answered in 50–60 minutes. Use elimination strategy and never leave blanks.

MCQ Format
📄

Paper 2 — Essay/Theory

Structured and essay questions. Marks are awarded for content, organisation, and language. Plan your answer before writing.

Written Answers
🔬

Paper 3 — Practical

For Science subjects only. Lab-based assessment marked separately. Practise experiments and know how to present results correctly.

Sciences Only
Study Tips

6 Strategies That Work for WAEC

01

Start with Past Questions

WAEC repeats question patterns consistently. Work through at least 5 years of past questions for each subject before the exam.

02

Master the Syllabus

Download the official WAEC syllabus and highlight topics you're weak on. WAEC only tests what's in the syllabus — nothing more.

03

Study English Every Day

English Language is compulsory and many students fail it. Practise comprehension, essays, and summary writing daily — even 30 minutes helps.

04

Create a Subject Rota

Don't cram all subjects in the final week. Create a 6-month study plan that covers each subject 2–3 times before the exam.

05

Use Group Study Wisely

Study in groups of 3–4 people. Quiz each other, teach topics you find easy, and clarify doubts together. Avoid groups that gossip.

06

Practice Timing

Simulate exam conditions at home. Set a timer and practise completing papers within the official time limit to build speed and confidence.

Subject-Specific Tips

Key Tips for Core Subjects

✍️

English Language

Focus on summary writing (know the format), comprehension (read the questions first), and essay (plan before you write). Read newspapers and novels regularly to improve your writing.

🔢

Mathematics

Practise every day — even just 10 questions. Focus on algebra, statistics, and geometry (highest-mark topics). Show all workings for essay questions even when you're unsure.

🌱

Biology

Draw and label diagrams — they carry significant marks. Learn biological processes as stories, not definitions. Practise classification questions from past papers.

⚗️

Chemistry

Memorise the periodic table groups, learn balancing equations, and practise all practical titration calculations. Theory questions often repeat verbatim from past papers.

Exam Day

Exam Day Checklist

🌙

The Night Before

  • Pack your bag with all required items
  • Confirm your exam centre address and time
  • Prepare your admission letter and ID
  • Sleep at least 7–8 hours
  • Avoid cramming past 10pm
  • Set two alarms
☀️

On Exam Day

  • Eat a light but filling breakfast
  • Arrive at the centre 30 minutes early
  • Read all instructions on the question paper first
  • Attempt all questions — no negative marking
  • Manage your time: don't spend too long on one question
  • Review your answers if time allows
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How many subjects do I need to pass for university admission?+

You need a minimum of 5 credits (grade C6 or above) including English Language and Mathematics. Many courses require credits in specific subjects — check the requirements for your chosen course at your chosen university.

Can I check my WAEC result online?+

Yes. Visit waecdirect.org and enter your exam number, exam year, and exam type. Results are usually released 45–90 days after the examination.

What happens if I fail a subject?+

You can re-sit as a private candidate in the next WAEC sitting (November/December). Your previous passes are not lost — you can combine results from different sittings. Contact WAEC directly for procedures.

Is the November/December WAEC accepted by universities?+

Yes, the Nov/Dec WAEC (for private candidates) is accepted by Nigerian universities. However, some institutions or courses may have specific requirements — always check with the institution directly.

Where do I register for WAEC as a private candidate?+

Visit the official WAEC website at waec.org.ng and follow the private candidate registration process. You'll need to visit a WAEC office or approved registration centre with your documents and passport photographs.
