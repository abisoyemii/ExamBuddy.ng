🔥 Latest Campus News — May 20, 2026

JAMB Extends Registration Deadline After Portal Outage

📅 May 20, 2026

JAMB has extended the 2026 registration deadline by 72 hours after the CAPS portal and payment system suffered repeated outages. Affected candidates now have extra time to complete registration without penalty.

What students need to know:

  • New deadline: May 23, 2026 at 11:59 PM
  • Students who started registration can resume from their saved progress
  • Keep your bank transaction reference ready for verification

Official advisory: JAMB asked candidates to use Chrome or Firefox and avoid mobile browsers during peak hours.

📈 Trending: #JambDeadline #CAPSRecovery

Federal Government Orders Freeze on New Hostel Fees

📅 May 19, 2026

The Federal Ministry of Education ordered universities to halt fresh hostel fee collection until outstanding accommodation allocations are fixed. This follows protests from new freshmen and student unions across major campuses.

Impact: Universities must release room assignments or refund fees by June 1.

📈 Trending: #HostelCrisis #NoNewFees

ASUU Warns of Strike If Funding Promise Is Broken

📅 May 18, 2026

ASUU has issued a 7-day warning strike notice demanding the release of owed allowances and funding for research. Lectures may be suspended if the government fails to act by May 25.

Student concerns: Academic delays and lost semester time.

📈 Trending: #ASUUStrike #SaveOurSemester

🏠 Accommodation Crisis: Students Left Homeless

Over 2,000 UNILAG Students Still Without Accommodation

May 3, 2026

Despite registering in time, over 2,000 newly admitted UNILAG students are yet to be allocated hostel accommodation. The university is "working on it."

What's happening: Students are sleeping in lecture halls, libraries, and friends' rooms while waiting for allocation.

Latest update: Management promised allocation by May 15. Students are skeptical.

OAU Accommodation: Students Pay Fees But Get No Rooms

May 2, 2026

OAU has collected hostel fees from 3,000+ students but hasn't allocated rooms. Many are protesting on social media.

Students' demands: Full refund or guaranteed accommodation by August 1.

📰 Trending School News

UI Lecturers Go on Strike Over Unpaid Allowances

May 4, 2026

University of Ibadan lecturers have suspended lectures indefinitely due to unpaid 3-month allowances. This affects 15,000 students currently on campus.

Impact: Classes suspended until at least May 20.

Students' reaction: Frustrated about academic calendar delays.

Nigerian Universities Ranked Low in Global Education Rankings

May 1, 2026

A new global university ranking places all Nigerian universities below 500th position. Only UNILAG and UI made the top 500 (barely).

Why: Poor research funding, inadequate facilities, brain drain of academics.

Universities affected: All Nigerian institutions

University Fees Increased Again - Another Burden for Poor Students

April 28, 2026

Most Nigerian universities have increased tuition fees by 25-40% for the 2026 academic session. This is the 3rd increase in 2 years.

New fee structure (approx):

  • Science programs: ₦300,000 - ₦500,000/year
  • Social sciences: ₦150,000 - ₦300,000/year
  • Professional programs: ₦600,000+/year

Poor students' plea: "This is unaffordable. Many will drop out."
