💡 Important: JAMB Score Is Only Half the Picture

UNILAG uses an aggregate scoring system that combines your JAMB score, Post-UTME score, and O'Level grades. A student with 260 and excellent O'Level results can beat a student with 280 and average grades. This means you must also excel in the Post-UTME and have strong WAEC/NECO credits in your relevant subjects.

UNILAG Post-UTME: What You Need to Know

After meeting UNILAG's JAMB cut-off, you must sit for the Post-UTME screening. UNILAG's Post-UTME is online and typically tests the same subjects as your JAMB exam. The aggregate formula used by UNILAG weights JAMB and Post-UTME scores together with O'Level results to produce a final admission score. Candidates are ranked by this aggregate — not JAMB alone.

Check UNILAG's official admissions portal at unilag.edu.ng and the JAMB CAPS portal once admissions open. Post-UTME forms are usually released 2–4 weeks after JAMB results.
