Complete Post-UTME Preparation Guide: How to Pass University Screening Exams

📅 Updated May 2026 | ⏱️ Read time: 12 minutes | 🎓 For: Post-UTME Candidates

So you've passed UTME. Congrats! But the real challenge is ahead: Post-UTME screening. This guide shows you exactly how to prepare and win.

❓ What is Post-UTME?

Post-UTME is a screening exam conducted by universities AFTER you've passed JAMB UTME. It's meant to further assess your suitability for your chosen program.

Key facts:

  • Conducted by individual universities (not JAMB)
  • Usually 2-4 months after UTME results
  • Test score + UTME = Final admission score
  • Typically worth 30% of final score (UTME is 70%)
  • If you fail post-UTME, you don't get admission (even with good UTME)

📊 How Post-UTME Score is Calculated

Component Weight Your Role
UTME Score (300 marks) 70% Already done - can't change
Post-UTME Score (100-300 marks) 30% This is what you prepare for NOW
Total Final Score 100% Determines if you get admitted

Example:

If you scored 250 in UTME and 80 in Post-UTME:

Final Score = (250 × 0.7) + (80 × 0.3) = 175 + 24 = 199/300

🎯 Why Post-UTME Matters

A good post-UTME score can:

  • ✓ Boost a borderline UTME score to admission
  • ✓ Move you up the merit list
  • ✓ Give you a better department/course option
  • ✓ Secure you scholarship (some schools use it)

📋 What Subjects Are Tested?

Post-UTME subjects depend on your program:

Science Programs (Medicine, Engineering, etc.)

  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Biology (for medicine) OR Mathematics (for engineering)

Social Sciences Programs (Law, Economics, etc.)

  • Government/Civics
  • English Language
  • Economics or History (depends on program)

Humanities Programs (Arts, Education, etc.)

  • Literature in English
  • History
  • Government or Other subject

⚠️ Check your university's post-UTME brochure for exact subjects!

🎓 Post-UTME Preparation Strategy

Step 1: Know Your Exam Format (CRITICAL!)

Before you start studying, understand:

  • How many questions? (usually 40-100)
  • What's the duration? (1-2 hours typically)
  • Is it multiple choice only or essay too?
  • What's the passing score? (varies by school)
  • Will past papers be available?

Action: Visit your university's admission portal and download the post-UTME brochure NOW.

Step 2: Identify Your Weakest Subject

  • Look at your UTME results
  • Which subject did you score lowest?
  • Is it being tested in post-UTME?
  • If yes, prioritize studying it intensely

Step 3: Create a 6-Week Study Plan

Sample Plan (if post-UTME is in August):

Week 1-2: Content Review

  • Review all topics from UTME curriculum
  • Read past exam questions to understand style
  • Identify frequently tested topics
  • Study 2-3 hours daily

Week 3-4: Deep Dive Practice

  • Solve past post-UTME questions daily
  • Time yourself - practice speed
  • Review mistakes carefully
  • Study 3-4 hours daily

Week 5: Mock Exams

  • Take full-length mock exams
  • Simulate actual exam conditions
  • Identify remaining weak areas
  • Study 4-5 hours daily

Week 6: Final Review

  • Light review of weak topics
  • Review formula and key concepts
  • Rest adequately (7-8 hours sleep)
  • Study 2 hours daily maximum

Step 4: Get Quality Study Materials

  • Past post-UTME papers (if available)
  • UTME past papers (similar difficulty)
  • Recommended textbooks for your subjects
  • Online resources and video tutorials

Step 5: Join a Study Group

  • Find 3-5 other candidates for your program
  • Meet once weekly to discuss tough topics
  • Quiz each other
  • Stay motivated and accountable

🔥 Pro Tips for High Post-UTME Scores

Tip #1: Post-UTME is Easier Than UTME

Good news! Post-UTME questions are typically less difficult than UTME. This is your chance to score higher percentage-wise.

Aim for: 70-80% (compared to UTME where 60% was good).

Tip #2: Focus on the Exact Subjects Your School Tests

Some schools test only 2 subjects (not 3). Don't waste time on subjects not being tested. Check your school's requirements!

Tip #3: Time Management is KEY

  • Post-UTME is usually shorter (1-2 hours)
  • You might have 40-50 questions in 1 hour
  • That's ~1.5 minutes per question
  • Practice solving questions quickly

Tip #4: Understand the Rubric (For Essay Questions)

If your post-UTME has essay questions:

  • Know how marks are distributed
  • Write to the rubric (don't go off-topic)
  • Quality > Quantity (better to answer 3 well than 5 poorly)
  • Use correct grammar and spelling

Tip #5: Review Past Post-UTME Papers (Gold!)

Most schools publish past papers. This is the best study tool:

  • Understand the question style and format
  • Identify question patterns
  • Some questions repeat year after year!
  • Solve 5-10 years of past papers

Tip #6: Don't Neglect English Language

Even for science programs, English questions appear. Master:

  • Grammar and sentence construction
  • Comprehension (reading and understanding)
  • This is an easy 10-15 points boost

Tip #7: Mental Preparation

  • Visualize yourself passing
  • Stay confident even if you scored low in UTME
  • Remember: Post-UTME is a second chance
  • Believe you can get 70%+ in post-UTME

❌ Common Post-UTME Mistakes

  • ❌ Starting preparation too late (start 6-8 weeks before)
  • ❌ Not checking what your school actually tests
  • ❌ Ignoring past papers (biggest mistake!)
  • ❌ Poor time management during the exam
  • ❌ Answering questions you don't understand
  • ❌ Leaving questions blank (always attempt all)
  • ❌ Not sleeping well before exam
  • ❌ Panicking if you don't know an answer

🚨 Exam Day Checklist

  • ✓ Sleep 8 hours the night before
  • ✓ Arrive at exam center 30 mins early
  • ✓ Bring: Admission letter, ID, pen, calculator (if allowed)
  • ✓ Eat a light meal before (avoid heavy food)
  • ✓ Stay calm and breathe deeply
  • ✓ Read all questions before answering
  • ✓ Answer easier questions first
  • ✓ Review your answers if time permits

💡 After Post-UTME: What's Next?

Once post-UTME is done:

  1. Results will be released in August-September
  2. Final merit list published (based on UTME + Post-UTME)
  3. If admitted: Complete registration immediately
  4. If not admitted: Check supplementary list (usually has more slots)
  5. Prepare for campus life and orientation

Bottom line: Post-UTME is your second chance to prove yourself. Don't waste it. Start preparing NOW, study consistently, and believe in yourself. Good luck!
