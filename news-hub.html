❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about admission processes and updates

Q: When will the supplementary admission list be released?

A: Most universities release supplementary lists 2-3 weeks after the main admission list. Check your institution's portal regularly for updates.

Q: What is the current JAMB cut-off mark for 2026?

A: The national minimum is 140, but most universities have cut-off marks between 180-250 depending on the program. Check your specific university here.

Q: How can I check my admission status?

A: Visit your university's admission portal (CAPS, JAMB, or university-specific portal) and log in with your JAMB registration number.

Q: Can I appeal my JAMB score?

A: Yes. JAMB allows score appeals within 2 weeks of result release. Visit the JAMB portal to submit an appeal if you believe your score is incorrect.

Q: What documents do I need for admission registration?

A: Most universities require: birth certificate, O-level results, UTME result printout, JAMB admission letter, and sometimes a passport photo. Check your specific university's requirements.
